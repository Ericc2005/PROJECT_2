How To Solve Cyberbullying

Go To: Home How To Solve Cyberbullying

If you see someone getting cyberbullied, there are many ways for you to be the solution to the problem. To get involved and help people avoid getting cyberbullied is to talk to the victim, see what the problem is, and solve it together. If this doesn’t work, contact any or other adults to help. If the bully is from the victim's school, contact the school and let them know what’s happening to stop this problem. Lastly, the best way to exceed this problem is to just ignore and block the cyberbully.

If you’re the one getting cyberbullied, there are many ways for you to solve this problem. If the bully is from your school then contact a trusted adult from school and let them know what’s happening and who’s doing it. If the bully is not from your school, still contact a trusted adult for help before the hate messages get consistent. Don’t give the bully attention by replying back with neither hate nor calm messages. The best and easiest way to avoid cyberbullying is to just not respond to the bully, ignore all hate messages, and block the bully online.

Statistics showing how the victims react to the harassment have also risen in a good way. Fortunately, 38% of cyberbullying victims admit to their parents about their harassment. Luckily, about 27% of cyberbullying victims admit to their friends about their harassment. The remaining 35% of cyberbullying victims don't admit to anyone about their harassment. Concluding this, cyberbullying is extremely atrocious and the best way to evade cyberbullying is to not give the bully attention.
